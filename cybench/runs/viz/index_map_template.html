CY-Bench dashboard

Benchmarking AI for global crop yield forecasting

CY-Bench is a common evaluation protocol for subnational maize and wheat yield forecasting. It compares statistical baselines, process-based crop models, conventional machine learning, deep sequence models, and tabular foundation models — not only on overall accuracy, but also on spatial, temporal, and anomaly skill.

Use the map to open country results, or jump to the global insight pages for cross-country summaries. Dataset and code: agml.org, ESSD paper, KDD branch.

Key findings

  • Feature-engineered ML and tabular foundation models achieve the strongest overall skill in most countries.
  • Spatial prediction is substantially easier than temporal and anomaly prediction across modeling paradigms.
  • Process-based models show comparatively strong temporal skill despite lower overall accuracy; year-to-year anomalies remain hard.
